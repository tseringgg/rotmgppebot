Shiny Items


Last updated: Exalt Version 6.2.0.0 (Oct 2025)





Shiny items are rare alternative versions of certain UT items (with the exception of a few select tiered items) that drop from the same locations as their regular counterparts at a much lower rate. All shiny items will appear in bags their regular variants drop in, with two exceptions being Kendo Stick and Jewel Eye Katana. They can only drop if you are playing on a Seasonal Character, but they can still be transferred to your regular Vault.



Shiny items’ sprites (along with the sprites of their projectiles, summons, etc. depending on their type) differ from their regular counterparts’ sprites and will play a “shine” effect animation when equipped. Additionally, shiny items are marked with a unique icon on the top left of their sprites. You can not forge shiny items, but you can still dismantle them for material if they are UT items.



Shiny items can be easily identified by , an icon that appears in the top left corner of an item.



Most batches of shiny items are added to the game with every new season of the Battlepass. Every batch listed below after the latest one is listed in chronological order starting from the first season in which shiny items were added.

History



Shiny items were introduced in Exalt Version 3.3.5.0 (Mar 2023).



Before Exalt Version 4.1.4.0 (Feb 2024), the legacy shiny variant of Sword of the Colossus was obtainable, and the shiny variant of Scepter of Devastation had the following sprite: 

 

The replacement of the legacy shiny variant of the Sword of the Colossus was due to a poll in the RotMG Discord List Discord server after many players complained about its sprite.

Trivia



    

  • Shiny items are most likely inspired by Shiny Pokemon, which are rare variants of creatures in the Pokemon franchise.
    • 

  • The Shiny Kendo Stick and Shiny Jewel Eye Katana are much rarer than other shinies.
    • 

  • The Kendo Stick most likely received a shiny sprite due to its legend of increasing drop rates when placed into the 7th slot of a player’s inventory.
    • 

  • The Shiny Jewel Eye Katana’s drop location is a reference to a bug that occurred around Summer in 2023 which prevented the normal Jewel Eye Katana from dropping from every possible location except for the Cave of a Thousand Treasures.
